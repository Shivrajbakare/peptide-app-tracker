GUIDED RECONSTITUTION TECHNIQUE
Step 1: Sanitize Work Space ▼
Clean a flat surface with an isopropyl alcohol wipe. Wash your hands thoroughly. Lay out your peptide vial, Bacteriostatic (BAC) water vial, syringe, and alcohol pads.
Step 2: Prep Vial Caps ▼
Pop off the plastic caps of both the peptide vial and the BAC water vial. Wipe the exposed rubber stoppers of both vials with a fresh alcohol pad. Allow them to air-dry for 10 seconds.
Step 3: Transfer BAC Water ▼
Draw the calculated amount of BAC water (e.g. 2.0ml) into a large syringe or standard insulin syringe. Insert the needle into the peptide vial stopper, and slowly depress the plunger along the glass wall. Do not inject directly into the powder to prevent cellular structure shearing.
Step 4: Dissolve & Store ▼
Remove the needle. Gently swirl the vial in circular motions until the lyophilized powder dissolves completely. Never shake the vial. Label with date and place in refrigeration (36°F–46°F / 2°C–8°C) immediately.
